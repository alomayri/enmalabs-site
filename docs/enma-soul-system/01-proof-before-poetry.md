# 01. Proof Before Poetry

The strongest repeated finding from the research lanes:

The site cannot rely on atmosphere alone. It needs one concrete proof surface
for Balsam before it earns broader studio language.

Required proof hierarchy:

1. Show the inner problem clearly.
2. Show how Balsam behaves in response.
3. Show what changes for the user.
4. Then let Enma Labs widen the frame.

Homepage proof requirements:

- The hero opens the room.
- The next surfaces must explain what Balsam actually helps with.
- The preview must feel like a believable product study, not a fictional UI world.
- The waitlist must clearly say what the user is joining and what happens next.
- The journal must feel real, linked, and authored.

Do not fake yet:

- invented testimonials
- fake download counts
- fake review stars
- fake partner logos
- fake product depth through made-up content

Acceptable current proof:

- specific product behaviors
- specific emotional outcomes
- real note pages
- concrete preview states
- clear “Balsam first” language
